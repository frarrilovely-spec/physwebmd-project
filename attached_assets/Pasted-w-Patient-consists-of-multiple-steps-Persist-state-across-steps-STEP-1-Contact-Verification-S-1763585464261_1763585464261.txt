w Patient consists of multiple steps. Persist state across steps.

STEP 1 – Contact Verification
- Screen title: “Verify your contact”
- User can choose verification via:
  - Email (default) OR
  - Phone (mobile)
- Field(s):
  - Email address OR Phone number (depending on selection)
- Button: “Send Code”
- After sending, show OTP screen:
  - Six input boxes for the code
  - Text: “We’ve sent a one-time passcode to [email/phone]. Enter it to continue.”
  - Button: “Continue”
  - Link: “Didn’t receive code? Resend.”

STEP 2 – Who are you seeking help for?
- Title: “Who are you seeking help for?”
- Two large selectable cards/buttons:
  - “Myself”
  - “Someone else”
- Button: “Next”
- Store selection as patientRelationshipContext.

STEP 3 – Your Information
- Title: “Your Information”
- Fields (all with clear labels and placeholders):
  - First Name (text)
  - Middle Name (text, optional)
  - Last Name (text)
  - Date of Birth (date picker or text input)
  - Cell Number (tel)
  - Gender (radio buttons):
    - Male
    - Female
- Primary Address group:
  - Street Name (text)
  - Apt, Suite, etc (text, optional)
  - City (text)
  - State (dropdown with US states – can mock list)
  - ZIP Code (text)
- “How did you hear about us?”:
  - Dropdown with options like:
    - Social Media
    - Friend/Family
    - Doctor Referral
    - Insurance Directory
    - Google Search
    - Other
- Button: “Continue”
- Basic validation: required fields should show inline error messages.

STEP 4 – Your Concern or Reason for Consultation
- Title: “Your concern or reason for consultation”
- Show a list of radio buttons (or multi-select checkboxes if you prefer). Use these options:
  - Anxiety / Panic Symptoms
  - Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
  - Bipolar Mood Disorder
  - Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  - Depressive Symptoms / Major Depression
  - Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)
  - Postpartum Depression / Perinatal Mood Concerns
  - I’m Not Sure – I would like an assessment
  - Eating Disorder / Disordered Eating Concerns
  - Personality Disorder or Personality-Related Difficulties
  - Substance Use / Addiction Concerns
  - Self-Harm Thoughts or Behaviors
  - Any Other – provide a multi-line text area
- Allow selecting at least one concern; if “Any Other” is chosen, the text area becomes required.
- Button: “Continue”

STEP 5 – Insurance Information
- Title: “Insurance Information”
- Fields:
  - Member First Name
  - Member Last Name
  - Member Date of Birth
  - Insurance Name
  - Insurance Member ID
  - Policy Holder (dropdown):
    - Me
    - Spouse
    - Parent
    - Step Parent
    - Other (when selected, show an extra text input)
- Button: “Continue”
- Optional: show subtle helper text: “We’ll verify your benefits before your appointment.”

STEP 6 – Mental Health Questions
- Title: “Let’s get some information about your mental health”
- Short consent text: “I agree to provide this information in upcoming screens.”
  - Implement as a checkbox that must be checked to continue.
- Questions:
  1) “Have you been admitted to a hospital or inpatient facility for mental health concerns within the past year?”
     - Yes / No (radio)
  2) “Do you currently feel at risk of harming yourself?”
     - Yes / No (radio)
     - If Yes, show a red banner: “If you are in crisis or considering harming yourself, please call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. This online booking system is not for emergencies.”
  3) “Please share about your relationship with alcohol:”
     - Dropdown with options:
       - After drinking alcohol, I feel healthy
       - After drinking alcohol, I feel unhealthy
       - I am unsure
       - I do not drink alcohol
  4) “Do you use drugs?”
     - Yes / No (radio)
     - If Yes, show an additional dropdown:
       - Daily
       - Once a week
       - Once a month
       - Occasionally
       - I have used drugs in the past but not currently
- Button: “Continue”

STEP 7 – Emergency Contact
- Title: “Emergency Contact Details”
- Fields:
  - First Name
  - Middle Name (optional)
  - Last Name
  - Relationship (dropdown with):
    - Caregiver
    - Friend
    - Parent
    - Spouse
    - Child
    - Sibling
    - Other (with text input if chosen)
  - Phone Number
- Button: “Continue”

STEP 8 – Appointment Scheduling
- Title: “Schedule your appointment”
- Show:
  - Visit type (In-person, Telehealth) – radio buttons.
  - Date picker or a simple mocked calendar.
  - List of available time slots (mock data; e.g., “9:00 AM, 10:30 AM, 2:00 PM”).
  - Optional “Preferred provider” dropdown (e.g., “First available”, “Dr. Smith”, etc. using mock names).
- After selecting date + time (and maybe provider), enable “Continue”.

STEP 9 – Review & Confirm
- Title: “Review your details”
- Show a summary card with all collected information:
  - Name, DOB, contact, address
  - Who they’re seeking help for
  - Concerns selected
  - Insurance info
  - Mental health questionnaire answers
  - Emergency contact
  - Appointment date, time, type, provider
- Each section should have a small “Edit” link that jumps back to that step.
- At bottom:
  - Checkbox list for:
    - “I agree to the Terms & Conditions.”
    - “I acknowledge the Privacy Policy / HIPAA Notice.”
    - “I consent to receive appointment reminders via email/SMS.”
  - Primary button: “Confirm Appointment”

STEP 10 – Confirmation Screen
- Title: “Appointment Request Submitted”
- Show:
  - Thank you message with patient’s first name.
  - Appointment details (date, time, type, provider).
  - Text explaining: “A confirmation email and SMS will be sent to you with appointment details and, if telehealth, a secure video link.”
- Buttons:
  - “Back to Home”
  - Optionally “Book Another Appointment”
